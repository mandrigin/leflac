# LE FLAC 1.4.0 — Training profiles, live queue, mix heat

LF-1 now understands that “gym-friendly” is not one genre, exposes the future
it is actually going to play, and learns which parts of a long mix you return
to.

## Activity-aware training

The old flat/loud score is replaced by four evidence profiles:

- **CARDIO** rewards trustworthy tempo, pulse contrast, steady drive, and few
  long breaks.
- **WEIGHTS** rewards low-end impact, transients, contrast, and sustained force.
- **GRIT** rewards aggression and relentless full-band drive. `.grift` remains
  accepted as an alias.
- **STATIC** favors stable, moderate, lower-jolt music for holds and mobility.

Enter `.gym` to switch profiles in place, or use `.cardio`, `.weights`, `.grit`,
or `.static` directly. Rows print a mode score, the visible qualified catalog is
the playback catalog, and the active mode appears in the system strip. Global
listening heat contributes only an 8% ranking tie-breaker after eligibility.
Typing `.` now opens an upward command layer; partial prefixes filter it and
each 48 dp suggestion is tappable and accessibility-labeled. Ordinary song and
mix matches use the same upward layer, keeping results clear of the keyboard.

The release also repairs the analysis foundation: native FFT source/scratch
aliasing, stereo downmix, FFT-window timestamps, rhythmic confidence, onset and
transient features, and stale analysis/profile migration. The v9 analysis cache
and v4 profile store are rebuilt automatically in the background after upgrade.

## The real Up Next

`NEXT nn` is no longer empty until a manual hold. It shows the effective Media3
future in exact order:

```text
CURRENT | HELD PRIORITY | GENERATED ORDER/RNG RAIL
```

Every row has an occurrence-specific `[X]`, so repeated tracks can be managed
without ambiguity. `[CLR HELD]` keeps its lossless meaning and preserves the
generated rail. Promoted tracks do not appear twice even though LF-1 retains a
temporary natural copy internally until priority playback consumes it.

## Hot segments inside mixes

Long-mix listening is now counted inside detected cue boundaries, including in
background/service-owned playback. Exposure is normalized by segment length
and whole-mix exposure: one ordinary full pass creates no heat, while replayed
or deliberately targeted segments become HOT.

HOT segments are drawn as bands on the scrubber, counted in mix telemetry, and
the cue ladder uses its third slot for the hottest learned jump elsewhere in
the set. Cue-map refinements rebind existing listening totals by interval
overlap instead of throwing history away.
